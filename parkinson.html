    What is Parkinson’s Disease?

    Parkinson’s disease is a progressive neurological condition caused by the gradual breakdown of nerve cells in the brain responsible for producing dopamine. Dopamine plays a key role in regulating movement, and as the brain’s ability to produce dopamine decreases, symptoms begin to appear.

    Common Symptoms of Parkinson’s Disease

    Many individuals experience symptoms before being diagnosed. The three primary symptoms of Parkinson’s include:

    • Slowed movement.
    • Involuntary shaking or tremors.
    • Stiff and inflexible muscles.

    Parkinson’s affects each person differently, with over 40 potential symptoms. Additional physical and psychological symptoms include:

    • Sleep disturbances.
    • Cognitive challenges.
    • Balance issues.
    • Urinary or bowel difficulties.
    • Loss of sense of smell.
    • Difficulty with swallowing, chewing, and speaking.
    • Depression and anxiety.
