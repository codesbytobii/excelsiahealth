    Your Medical and Birth Tourism Journey with Excelsia Health Travels.

    At Excelsia Health Travels, we understand that the quality of healthcare is paramount, whether you're traveling for medical treatments or birth tourism. We are committed to providing a smooth, safe, and stress-free experience, ensuring that you receive top-tier medical care or maternity services.

    How We Research and Select Hospitals

    Accreditation and Certification

    We only collaborate with internationally accredited hospitals, ensuring they meet global standards for patient safety and healthcare quality.

    Specialist Expertise

    We work with hospitals that have highly qualified, board-certified specialists with extensive experience in the procedures or maternity care you need.

    State-of-the-Art Facilities

    We evaluate the hospital's technology, equipment, and infrastructure to ensure they offer the most advanced treatments and support for deliveries.

    Patients safety and Outcome

    We review each hospital’s patient safety protocols, success rates, and track record of positive outcomes for both medical treatments and childbirth.

    Cost Transparency

    We prioritize hospitals that provide clear, upfront pricing, ensuring that there are no hidden costs for treatments, surgeries, or services.

    Multilingual Staff

    Hospitals with English-speaking staff or access to interpreters are preferred to ensure effective communication throughout your treatment or journey.

    The Process: How We Facilitate Your Medical or Birth Tourism Journey

    Our step-by-step process is designed to make your medical or birth tourism journey as smooth and hassle-free as possible, ensuring that you receive world-class care and support from start to finish.

    • 1. Step 1: Free Consultation and Assessment
      • You contact us via phone, email, or through our website to schedule a free initial consultation.
      • Our experts assess your healthcare or maternity needs, review your medical reports or prenatal care details, and answer any questions you have.
      • We recommend the most suitable destinations and hospitals based on your diagnosis, budget, and personal preferences (for medical tourism) or birthright citizenship opportunities (for birth tourism).
    • 2. Step 2: Research and Treatment or Birth Planning
      • We conduct thorough research on the best hospitals and specialists in your selected destination.
      • For medical tourism, we provide you with multiple options for hospitals and specialists, including detailed profiles of the facilities, doctors, and treatment costs.
      • For birth tourism, we recommend top maternity hospitals and prenatal care providers, outlining their expertise in handling pregnancies and deliveries.
      • Together, we finalize your treatment plan or birth plan, selecting the most appropriate hospital and procedure.
    • 3. Step 3: Travel and Visa Arrangements
      • We assist in securing a medical or tourist visa for your destination, helping with document preparation, appointment letters, and visa application submissions.
      • We arrange your flight bookings, ensuring you have access to the best routes, affordable rates, and flexible options.
      • We secure accommodation close to the hospital, customized to your preferences—whether you prefer luxury, budget-friendly, or recovery-friendly stays.
      • We also handle airport transfers and local transportation to ensure you have a seamless transition from the airport to your medical facility or maternity hospital.
    • 4. Step 4: Pre-Treatment or Pre-Birth Coordination
      • Before your departure, we coordinate with the hospital or maternity center to schedule all your appointments, procedures, and consultations.
      • We provide you with a detailed itinerary, outlining every aspect of your medical treatment or birth tourism journey, including pre-operative consultations for medical procedures or prenatal check-ups for birth tourism.
    • 5. Step 5: Treatment, Delivery, and Recovery
      • Upon arrival at your destination, our local partners and hospital staff ensure that your treatment or maternity process begins smoothly.
      • For medical tourism, we remain in constant communication with the hospital to monitor your treatment progress and ensure you receive the best care.
      • For birth tourism, we stay in close contact with the hospital to ensure your prenatal, delivery, and postnatal care is handled with the highest standards.
      • We assist in arranging rehabilitation, recovery services, and postnatal care, whether you remain abroad or return home.
    • 6. Step 6: Post-Treatment or Post-Delivery Care and Follow-Up
      • After you return to Nigeria, we ensure you have access to local follow-up care, as recommended by your medical specialist or maternity provider.
      • We coordinate with your local doctors to ensure ongoing treatment or postnatal care is aligned with the care you received abroad.
      • For medical tourism, we provide support for any additional travel or follow-up consultations, if needed.
      • For birth tourism, we assist in securing birth certificates, passports, and citizenship paperwork for your newborn.

    Visa Application Process for most Destinations

    When traveling abroad for medical treatment or birth tourism, obtaining the correct visa is crucial. We assist with visa applications for various countries, ensuring smooth and timely approvals. Below is a detailed breakdown of the visa application process for some popular medical and birth tourism destinations.

    • 1. India Medical Visa

      India is one of the top medical tourism destinations, offering affordable and world-class treatments. The Indian government provides a dedicated medical visa (M-Visa) for patients traveling to India for specialized medical care.

      Application Process:

      1. Gather Required Documents:
        • A valid passport (minimum 6 months validity).
        • Completed online visa application form on the Indian visa website.
        • Recent passport-sized photographs.
        • Referral letter from your country-of-origin doctor for treatment in India.
        • Appointment/acceptance letter from the Indian hospital.
        • Proof of financial means to cover medical expenses (bank statements).
        • Medical history and reports detailing your health condition.
      2. Visa Appointment:
        • Schedule an appointment with the Indian Visa Application Centre (IVAC) in your country of origin.
      3. Submit Application:
        • Submit the required documents and biometric data at the IVAC.
      4. Medical Visa Approval: The visa allows multiple entries to India, typically for up to 6 months, with the possibility of extension depending on the treatment.
    • 2. Turkey Medical Visa

      Turkey has become a popular destination for both medical tourism and birth tourism, providing high-quality care at affordable prices. For medical tourists, a Short-Term Medical Visa is required.

      Application Process:

      1. Prepare Required Documents:
        • Valid passport (at least six months' validity).
        • Completed Turkey visa application form.
        • Two recent passport-sized photos.
        • Medical invitation letter from a hospital in Turkey or maternity facility.
        • Proof of sufficient funds to cover medical or maternity expenses (bank statements).
        • Proof of accommodation and travel insurance covering medical costs.
      2. Visa Application Submission:
        • Submit your application through the Turkey Visa Application Centre (VAC) or apply for an e-Visa online if eligible.
      3. Medical or Birth Visa Approval: Visa approval usually takes 7 to 10 business days. Once approved, you can travel for your treatment or delivery.
    • 3. United Arab Emirates (Dubai) Medical Treatment Visa

      Dubai offers a Medical Treatment Visa for international patients. The UAE Medical Treatment Visa allows individuals to visit Dubai or other Emirates for medical care or childbirth.

      Application Process:

      1. Gather Required Documents:
        • Valid passport (minimum six months validity).
        • Completed medical visa application form.
        • A letter from a licensed healthcare facility in Dubai confirming your treatment or delivery plan.
        • Proof of financial means to cover treatment or maternity services.
        • Recent passport-sized photographs.
        • Proof of travel insurance covering medical expenses.
      2. Sponsor Hospital Submission: The hospital in Dubai serves as your visa sponsor and must apply for the visa on your behalf through the Dubai Health Authority (DHA) portal.
      3. Visa Issuance: Once processed, you will receive a single-entry visa valid for 90 days, extendable if necessary.
    • 4. South Africa Medical Visa

      South Africa is a popular destination for Nigerians seeking advanced medical treatments. A Medical Treatment Visa is required for those traveling for healthcare purposes.

      Application Process:

      1. Prepare Required Documents:
        • Completed South Africa medical visa application form.
        • Valid passport with at least 30 days of validity after the intended departure.
        • Two passport-sized photos.
        • A referral letter from your Nigerian/country-of-origin doctor.
        • A letter from a South African hospital outlining your treatment plan.
        • Proof of sufficient funds for your treatment and living expenses.
        • Proof of accommodation in South Africa.
      2. Submit Application: Submit the required documents in person at the South African Visa Application Centre in Nigeria.
      3. Medical Visa Approval: The visa is typically issued for the duration of the treatment and can be extended if additional care is required.
    • 5. United Kingdom Medical Visa

      The UK offers a Standard Visitor Visa for those traveling for short-term medical treatment or childbirth. This visa is suitable for both medical tourism and birth tourism purposes.

      Application Process:

      1. Gather Required Documents:
        • Completed UK visa application form.
        • Valid Nigerian passport.
        • Two passport-sized photographs.
        • Referral letter from your Nigerian doctor.
        • Invitation letter from a UK hospital or maternity facility outlining the treatment or birth plan.
        • Proof of funds to cover treatment, living expenses, and travel (bank statements).
        • Proof of accommodation during your stay.
      2. Apply Online: Complete the online application through the UK visa portal and pay the visa fee.
      3. Attend Visa Appointment: Schedule an appointment at the UK Visa Application Centre for biometric submission.
      4. Medical Visa Approval: The process typically takes around 15 working days. The visa is valid for six months and can be extended if necessary.
    • 6. Germany Medical Visa

      Germany offers a Medical Treatment Visa for patients seeking advanced healthcare. This visa allows patients to receive treatment in Germany’s leading hospitals and clinics.

      Application Process:

      1. Prepare Required Documents:
        • Valid Nigerian passport.
        • Completed visa application form.
        • Two passport-sized photos.
        • Referral letter from your Nigerian doctor.
        • A letter from a German hospital outlining your treatment plan.
        • Proof of sufficient funds for treatment and stay.
        • Travel insurance covering medical expenses.
      2. Submit Application: Submit the required documents and biometric data at the German Embassy in Nigeria.
      3. Visa Approval: The visa is issued for the duration of the treatment and can be extended if needed.
    • 7. Singapore Medical Visa

      Singapore allows patients to apply for a Short-Term Visit Pass for medical treatment or childbirth at renowned healthcare facilities.

      Application Process:

      1. Gather Required Documents:
        • Completed visa application form.
        • Valid Nigerian passport.
        • Two passport-sized photos.
        • Medical referral letter from your Nigerian doctor or prenatal care provider.
        • Appointment letter from a recognized hospital in Singapore.
        • Proof of sufficient funds to cover treatment or childbirth.
        • Travel insurance covering medical emergencies.
      2. Submit Application: Submit your visa application through the Singapore High Commission or via the Singapore Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA).
      3. Visa Issuance: Once approved, the Short-Term Visit Pass is valid for up to 90 days, with an option for extension.
    • 8. Brazil Medical Visa

      Brazil is known for medical and birth tourism, offering excellent medical care and birthright citizenship.

      Requirements:

      • Valid Passport: Minimum six months’ validity.
      • Visa Application Form: Completed visa application form.
      • Passport-Sized Photos: Two recent passport-sized photos.
      • Doctor’s Referral Letter: Referral from your Nigerian doctor for treatment or delivery in Brazil.
      • Hospital Appointment Letter: Confirmation from a Brazilian hospital.
      • Proof of Funds: Financial documents showing you can cover the medical or maternity expenses.
      • Accommodation Proof: Hotel booking or accommodation details in Brazil.
      • Travel Insurance: Insurance covering medical or maternity-related costs.

      Steps to Apply:

      • Submit your visa application through the Brazilian Embassy in Nigeria.
      • The visa typically allows multiple entries for up to 90 days, with the possibility of extension.
    • 9. Canada Medical Visa

      Canada offers a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) for medical treatment or birth tourism, allowing entry for healthcare purposes.

      Requirements:

      • Valid Passport: Nigerian passport with at least six months' validity.
      • Visa Application Form: Completed TRV visa application form.
      • Passport-Sized Photos: Two recent photos.
      • Doctor's Referral Letter: A referral letter from your Nigerian doctor.
      • Hospital Invitation Letter: An invitation from a Canadian hospital outlining the treatment or delivery plan.
      • Proof of Funds: Bank statements or sponsorship letters showing sufficient financial means.
      • Accommodation Proof: Proof of accommodation arrangements during your stay.
      • Travel Insurance: Insurance covering medical or maternity-related expenses.

      Steps to Apply:

      • Submit your application online or through the Canada Visa Application Centre (VAC).
      • The visa is typically issued for up to 6 months, with the possibility of extensions.
    • 10. United States Medical Visa

      The US B-2 Tourist Visa is typically used for both medical tourism and birth tourism, allowing you to receive medical care or give birth in the United States.

      Requirements:

      • Valid Passport: Nigerian passport with at least six months’ validity.
      • Visa Application Form (DS-160): Completed online through the US Department of State’s website.
      • Passport-Sized Photos: Recent passport-sized photos.
      • Medical Treatment Letter: Referral letter from a Nigerian doctor and confirmation from a US hospital outlining the treatment or delivery plan.
      • Proof of Financial Means: Bank statements or financial sponsorship letters showing you can cover your medical or maternity expenses.
      • Accommodation Details: Proof of where you will stay during your time in the US.
      • Medical Reports: Detailed medical history and treatment plan.
      • Visa Fee Payment: Payment of the visa fee.

      Steps to Apply:

      • Complete the DS-160 online visa application and schedule an interview at the US Embassy or Consulate in Nigeria.
      • Attend your visa appointment for document submission and biometric collection.
      • The B-2 Visa is typically issued for six months and can be extended if additional treatment or time for recovery is needed.
    • 11. Mexico Medical Visa

      Mexico is a growing hub for medical and birth tourism, offering affordable healthcare services and birthright citizenship.

      Requirements:

      • Valid Passport: Minimum six months’ validity.
      • Visa Application Form: Completed visa application form.
      • Passport-Sized Photos: Two recent photos.
      • Doctor’s Referral Letter: Referral letter from a Nigerian doctor for medical treatment or delivery in Mexico.
      • Hospital Invitation Letter: Confirmation from a Mexican hospital.
      • Proof of Funds: Financial documents showing ability to cover treatment and travel.
      • Accommodation Proof: Proof of accommodation arrangements.
      • Travel Insurance: Insurance covering medical and maternity expenses.

      Steps to Apply:

      • Submit your visa application through the Mexico Visa Application Centre or consulate in Nigeria.
      • The visa is typically issued for 90 days and allows multiple entries.
    • 12. Argentina Medical Visa (Medical and Birth Tourism)

      Argentina is an emerging destination for birth tourism due to birthright citizenship and medical tourism for affordable treatments.

      Requirements:

      • Valid Passport: Minimum six months’ validity.
      • Visa Application Form: Completed visa application form.
      • Passport-Sized Photos: Two recent photos.
      • Doctor’s Referral Letter: Referral letter from your Nigerian doctor.
      • Hospital Invitation Letter: Confirmation from an Argentinian hospital or maternity clinic.
      • Proof of Funds: Bank statements showing financial means to cover the treatment or maternity care.
      • Accommodation Proof: Proof of accommodation in Argentina.
      • Travel Insurance: Insurance covering medical or maternity costs.

      Steps to Apply:

      • Submit your visa application through the Argentinian Embassy in Nigeria.
      • The visa typically allows a stay of 90 days, extendable if necessary for medical reasons.
    • 13. General Visa Application Guidelines for Medical and Birth Tourism from Nigeria

      Document Checklist for All Destinations:

      • Valid passport (at least six months validity).
      • Completed visa application forms.
      • Recent passport-sized photographs.
      • Medical reports or prenatal care records and referral letters.
      • Official invitation letter from the hospital abroad, detailing the treatment or delivery plan.
      • Proof of sufficient funds (bank statements) or sponsorship to cover medical/maternity costs.
      • Proof of accommodation (hotel bookings or letters from family/friends abroad).
      • Travel insurance covering medical or childbirth emergencies.

      Steps to Apply:

      • Submit your visa application through the Argentinian Embassy in Nigeria.
      • The visa typically allows a stay of 90 days, extendable if necessary for medical reasons.
